Fyodor Dostoevsky

Russian Author

Fyodor Dostoesky was a Russian author born in November 11th, 1821 and died on

February 9th, 1881. He has written a lot of books one of his most recognized works is

titled “Crime and Punishment” this book is about how a man was driven to commit a

double homicide. A lot of his works contain some truth to his life and what was

currently happening in his life. He had traumatic experiences he was part of a group

and they got arrested in a Russian prison which were really bad conditions and they

were treated badly he watched some of his fellow friends die right in front of him.

After some time he got married but a few years later his wife died. Fyodor was forced

to join the Siberian Army Corps of the Seventh Line Battalion. Being forced to join a

Siberian Army Corps affected hm in the long run he had a deep understanding of

human psychology he implanted this in his books which made them one of a kind at

that time.
